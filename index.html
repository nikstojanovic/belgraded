Exchange foreign currency in Belgrade

We are not a part of European Union, our currency is Serbian dinar (short “RSD”).

If you try to pay a product or service in foreign currency, some people may accept Euro, US dollar or Swiss franc, but exchange rate will probably not be in your favour.

Foreign currencies that are usually accepted by exchange offices are Euro, American dollar and Swiss franc. If you use other currencies, don’t be confused if you get rejected. You will have to try to do the exchange in a bank.

Taxi will cost you about 2000.00RSD to get from the airport to the city. Ticket for A1 shuttle bus is 300.00RSD. There are a lot exchange offices in the city, and their exchange rate is not bad.

Serbian Post Office offers good exchange rate. Downside: sometimes it gets crowded so you will have to wait in line. You can see location of post offices here. You can see their current exchange rate here.

A1 bus line (Belgrade airport to Slavia square)

Driver will sell you the ticket - price is 300.00RSD. From 7.20AM to 18:40PM frequency of this line is 20 minutes. However, between 19.00 and 06.00 bus line is less frequent, so you may want to use a taxi. It takes 45 to 60 minutes to get from airport to downtown by bus.

Public transport in Belgrade

We have buses, trams and trolleybuses. Tickets are valid for all three types of vehicles.

Belgrade public transport is divided in 4 zones. You'll probably need just Zone 1 and Zone 2. If you decide to visit Avala hill it's in Zone 2, so you are covered. You can see detailed map of Zones here. Zone 1 is shown in red, Zone 2 in yellow, Zone 3 in green, Zone 4 in blue.

If you ride without a ticket, fine is 2000.00RSD if you have the money to pay immediately, and 6000.00RSD if you don't. In that case, fine will be sent to your address by mail.

Local public transportation system is called Busplus. You buy a ticket at a kiosk located near bus stops in town center. Recommended option is whole day ticket:

These tickets are NOT valid from 00.00AM to 04.00AM. If you decide to ride between 00.00AM and 04.00AM you'll have to buy a single ticket from the driver, price is 150.00RSD for Zone 1 and 210.00RSD for Zone 2. Friendly suggestion, use a taxi between 00.00 and 04.00AM, buses are very rare this late.

You can also buy a ticket that you can add credit to. This ticket costs 250.00RSD initially, and then you add credit to it. When you validate your card in the vehicle, ride costs 89.00RSD, and you can change vehicles and direction of travel during a 90 minute period.

Lastly, you can buy ticket from the driver and it costs 150.00RSD. You need to prepare exact amount of cash, and it is time consuming. I highly suggest not to buy ticket this way.
